SwimAnalytics vs Strava

What Strava Does Well

  • Social features: Clubs, segments, kudos, activity feed
  • Multi-sport tracking: Running, cycling, swimming, hiking, etc.
  • Free tier: Generous free features for casual athletes
  • Massive user base: Connect with millions of athletes worldwide
  • Apple Watch integration: Direct sync from workouts

What SwimAnalytics Does Better

  • Swimming-specific metrics: CSS, sTSS, pace zones designed for pools
  • Training load analysis: CTL/ATL/TSB included (Strava doesn't have this)
  • Automatic sTSS: No manual data entry, calculated from CSS + pace
  • Stroke mechanics: DPS, stroke rate, stroke index tracking
  • Training zones: 7 personalized pace zones based on your physiology

Verdict: SwimAnalytics vs Strava

Use Strava if: You want social features, multi-sport tracking, or free casual tracking. Strava is excellent for logging workouts and connecting with friends.

Use SwimAnalytics if: You're serious about swimming performance and want CSS-based zones, automatic sTSS, and training load management (CTL/ATL/TSB). Strava doesn't calculate swimming TSS or provide PMC metrics.

Use both: Many swimmers use Strava for social sharing and SwimAnalytics for performance tracking. They complement each other well.
