About SwimAnalytics

Science-based swimming performance tracking, built by swimmers for swimmers

SwimAnalytics brings professional-grade performance tracking to every swimmer. We believe that advanced metrics like Critical Swim Speed (CSS), Training Stress Score (TSS), and Performance Management Charts shouldn't be locked behind expensive platforms or require complex coaching software.

Scientific Foundation

SwimAnalytics is built on decades of peer-reviewed sports science research:

Critical Swim Speed (CSS)

Based on Wakayoshi et al. (1992-1993) research at Osaka University. CSS represents the theoretical maximum swimming velocity sustainable without exhaustion, corresponding to lactate threshold.

Key Research: Wakayoshi K, et al. "Determination and validity of critical velocity as an index of swimming performance." European Journal of Applied Physiology, 1992.

Training Stress Score (TSS)

Adapted from Dr. Andrew Coggan's cycling TSS methodology for swimming. Quantifies training load by combining intensity (relative to CSS) and duration.

Key Research: Coggan AR, Allen H. "Training and Racing with a Power Meter." VeloPress, 2010. Adapted for swimming by SwimAnalytics using CSS as threshold.

Performance Management Chart (PMC)

Chronic Training Load (CTL), Acute Training Load (ATL), and Training Stress Balance (TSB) metrics. Tracks fitness, fatigue, and form over time.

Implementation: 42-day exponentially weighted moving average for CTL, 7-day for ATL. TSB = CTL - ATL.

SWOLF & Stroke Metrics

Swimming efficiency metrics combining time and stroke count. Used by elite swimmers and coaches worldwide to track technical improvements.

Standard Metrics: SWOLF = Time + Strokes. Lower scores indicate better efficiency. Supplemented by Distance Per Stroke (DPS) and Stroke Rate (SR).
